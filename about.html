About Me

I build clean, accessible frontend solutions to foster an inclusive digital landscape.

My Story

I am a frontend engineer driven by a passion for accessibility, user experience, and semantic engineering. Over the past few years, my work has focused on bridging the gap between artistic web UI development and functional, inclusive accessibility compliance.

I believe that the web should be usable by everyone, regardless of cognitive, physical, visual, or situational impairments. Consequently, I dedicate my development practices to strict adherence to the W3C Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG 2.1 AA and AAA), writing structured semantic HTML, and managing keyboard tab loops.

When I'm not coding, I run accessibility audits on open source projects, consult on keyboard focus trap optimizations, and experiment with low-latency client-side frameworks.
